Biography

A Concert musician, author and composer, Sherif is a multiple international prize winner, including the 2024 Platinum Absolute First Prize of the prestigious World Master Open Music Competition, the 2024 Platinum Absolute First Prize and the "Artist of the month" special award of the European Classical Music - Strings Award, the first prize winner of the 4th Lugano International Music Competition, the first prize of the "London Classical Music Competition", the Silver prize of the "International Mozart Competition Vienna", and the 2023 EMA music award. He is the 2022 unanimous laureate of the Concert prize and the 2017 Virtuosity prize of the Schola Cantorum. In 2009, he moved to Paris with a scholarship from the Ecole Normale de Musique de Paris after his graduation from the London Royal Academy of Music. Dahroug’s latest album, The Art of Variations, is a live recording published under the Lyre de Mercure - Label, and featuring the artist interpreting a trinity of music masterpieces that figures high on the guitar and music literature pantheon. Critics describe the album as a "Magnificent, insightful and powerful musical interpretation, each note, each pluck of the strings, becomes a brushstroke on the canvas of sound, a true treasure contributing to the universal cultural heritage and accretion to the French National Biblioteca. " Dahroug's musical composition range extends beyond traditional genres, spanning from neo-impressionism to theatrical plays' sound design. His compositions exhibit versatility in style and form. Sherif's latest release, "Eirene - Old Comedy! A Bi-tonal, quasi-new age Fantasia," comprises two movements designed to accompany Aristophanes' enigmatic play.
